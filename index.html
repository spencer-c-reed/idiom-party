👅 TIP OF THE TONGUE
🤪

Sounds wrong.
Feels right.

Race through two kinds of warped sayings: Synonym Twists and Mixed Messages.

How to play

  1. Split into two teams. On each turn, a reader from the opposing team holds the phone and keeps score.
  2. Synonym Twist: the saying is rebuilt with indirect substitutes. Example: “outer edge of your mouth muscle” means “tip of your tongue.”
  3. Mixed Message: a riddle-like description points to the whole saying. Both styles are mixed into one deck.
  4. The reader says only what is shown. Only the active team guesses; close wording that clearly identifies the standard phrase counts.
  5. Only the active team may guess. Tap Got it! for a point. You get two skips per turn; revealing an answer uses a skip and earns no point.
  6. When time runs out, swap roles. Both teams always get the same number of turns; the higher score after reaching the target wins.
